Designed and 3D printed a box for UNO cards Personal project

I was recently gifted UNO cards and introduced to the game, and I’ve been having a lot of fun with it. I did not have a good way to store the cards, so I decided to dust up my CAD knowledge and design and 3D print a box for them.

I first measured the dimensions of the cards (length and width) and the height of the stack. Since this was the first time I used a 3D printer, I left a ~7-8% buffer. This of course meant that the final result ended up being a little big for the cards, but as a first try I wanted to make sure the box is big enough.Â 

After taking the measurements I designed a box with the appropriate dimensions in SolidWorks. I then cut out half-circles on the long walls and half-ovals on the short ones for two reasons: making it easier to place the deck inside (instead of just dropping it in), and to cut down on materials.

Afterwards created a basic lid for the box. To make it a little more interesting and again, to save down on materials I decided to use the UNO logo oval as an opening in the lid. To do this accurately, I found an image of the logo online, took the oval from there and used it on the model directly to create the opening in the lid.

The completed box ended up working well enough, the cards fit, and the buffer isn’t too big. It needs some sanding to ease up the edges though. As a first 3D project I wanted to make my own design entirely, but for a potential second revision I would look into closing mechanisms I can utilise, and of course minimise the buffer.
