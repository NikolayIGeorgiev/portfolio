IML in Physiotherapy Master's thesis

Context

This project is my master’s thesis (30 credits). I designed a system that is aiming to assist physiotherapists in their practice, by utilising interactive machine learning (IML). This work also serves as a pre-study for a research project with the same objective, which will be developed in the department.

Role

I conducted literature review on the topic of interactive machine learning and its open-source software implementations, and the practice of physiotherapy. I designed and conducted interviews with physiotherapists, in order to focus on the specific issues they are facing. From those interviews I ideated solutions and developed them into storyboards. After approval from the physiotherapists, I created pen-and-paper prototypes and interactive Figma prototypes for the interface of the system I proposed, which were also evaluated after each iteration.

Overview

With this thesis I created a proof of concept for a system that can learn how a patient is performing a rehabilitation exercise to the best of their current ability (under physiotherapist supervision and with instruction). Afterwards it will be able to track whether the patient is using proper form and the appropriate range of motion when they are doing their prescribed exercise routine.

Methods: Brainstorming (and bodystorming), Interviews (writing and conducting), Thematic analysis, Literature review, Low fidelity prototypes (paper, wireframes, digital), High fidelity prototypes (wireframes), User evaluations, Technological exploration.

Keywords

  • Machine learning
