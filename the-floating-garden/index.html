The Floating Garden Course project

Prototype interface

Ideation

Context

This group project was done for the university course Advanced Interaction Design (5 credits). The teacher partnered with a local housing company, which was looking for ways to inform tenants about their power usage. At the end of the course, our design was one of the few chosen to be presented to the company.

Role

I initiated a conversation within the team about the definition of power in physics and how it is different from energy, and made sure we used the proper concepts and terminology throughout our design. This led our graphic designer to come up with the metaphor of a waterfall and pipes bursting when too much power was being used. I also introduced the idea of using an electronic paper display, after some technological exploration, in order to keep up with the spirit of the project. Otherwise, I was mostly involved with planning the logic and functionality of the design and constructing the Figma prototype. I participated in the research stage, contributing to the bedrock of our ideas and wrote parts of the final report.

Overview

This design is supposed to track the power usage of each apartment and, as long as the value does not reach a certain threshold at the peak hours, a plant will grow fully for the day, leading to a rich garden after each month. The tenants would be able to monitor their usage and adjust it accordingly, assisted by tips.

Methods: Brainstorming, Personas, Interviews (writing and conducting), Thematic analysis, Literature review, Low fidelity prototypes (paper, wireframes), High fidelity prototypes (wireframes).
